# Holistic MEAL Framework Development — Urban Pathways

### Overview

This project involved the design and development of a comprehensive Monitoring, Evaluation, Accountability, and Learning (MEAL) Framework for a Housing First-aligned program supporting individuals and households experiencing housing instability.

The framework was developed as part of a professional assignment for Urban Pathways and aimed to strengthen evidence-based programming, adaptive management, accountability systems, and outcome measurement across integrated social support services.

Following the completion of the assignment process, I was offered a Program Evaluator position with Urban Pathways.

### Project Objectives

The framework was designed to:

- Support long-term housing stability and improved well-being
- Strengthen monitoring and outcome tracking systems
- Improve accountability and service responsiveness
- Integrate learning and adaptive programming mechanisms
- Capture both quantitative and qualitative outcomes
- Support data-informed decision-making across multidisciplinary teams

### Key Components of the Framework

**1. Results-Based Monitoring Structure**

Developed a structured framework connecting:

- Domains
- Outcomes
- Objectives
- Indicators
- Measurement tools
- Data sources
- Data disaggregation categories
- Responsible staff
- Use of findings
- Learning and adaptation notes

**2. Integrated Program Domains**

The framework covered multiple interconnected sectors, including:

- Permanent Housing Stability
- Safety & Risk Reduction
- Physical Health
- Nutrition & Food Security
- Substance Use & Recovery Support
- Mental & Emotional Well-being
- Psychosocial Integration
- Life & Soft Skills
- Income & Financial Stability
- Employability Skills
- Education & Training
- Program Satisfaction & Feedback
- Client Engagement & Participation

**3. Quantitative & Qualitative Methodologies**

Integrated mixed-method approaches including:

**Quantitative Tools**
- Surveys
- Attendance tracking
- Standardized assessments
- Program records
- HMIS data
- Clinical records
- Payroll and employment tracking

**Qualitative & Participatory Approaches**
- Focus Group Discussions (FGDs)
- Case notes
- Client feedback mechanisms
- Reflection sessions
- Storytelling and lived-experience documentation
- Staff observations
- Participatory engagement tracking

**Accountability & Learning Approach**

The framework embedded accountability and adaptive learning mechanisms through:

- Client-centered feedback systems
- Service satisfaction monitoring
- Early warning and intervention indicators
- Continuous reflection and service improvement
- Use-of-findings integration for adaptive programming
- Disaggregated analysis to identify vulnerable groups and inequities

**Data Disaggregation**

Indicators were disaggregated by categories such as:

- Age
- Gender
- Housing type
- Risk level
- Disability status
- Education level
- Chronic illness
- Mental health diagnosis
- Service intensity
- Food security status

This supported equity-focused analysis and targeted interventions.
